Craft

That --> This:

 

I wanted to add on to my original "STUDIES" website. I intended the new work to be a part of the STUDIES site yet be a distinct branching off from the self-reflexivity and immediate reporting of the STUDIES work itself. I wanted to maintain a continuity with my project and needed a couple of buckets full of new content so that I could give the viewer something new to engage in and demonstrate that this takes coding! For me, this website coding is like building - digital building. So what led me here? The easy answer could be "all of my previous experience".

Over the years I've been involved with a lot of creative projects of all kinds. I don't know what I'd do without a workbench nearby, honestly. For as long as I can remember I've either been drawing something, building or fixing something. Wait a minute! - the photo archives - a place for content! Now I have enough photos, text and memories to make a lot of work for myself trying to create code to handle it all! For the purposes of this website the three "Cs" would be Concept, Cohesiveness and Coding - now that I've got content taken care of...

Various Works

Earlier in STUDIES on the first page of this website I implemented what I might refer to as 'image delivery' - a way to click on an image that returns some enhanced way of viewing that image. On that page you click and a much bigger image zooms in and the background gets draped with a kind of dark theater mode where you can focus on the image. You click to get out and you enter and exit images one by one. On this page I've used a similar javaScripted gallery however you can stay in there and go from image to image in the 'dark theater'. Additionally, there's a play button that initiates an automatic slideshow of the entirety of the images (some of which were taken before the advent of digital photography!).
